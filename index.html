About Me

PhD student in Chester F. Carlson Center for Imaging Science at Rochester Institute of Technology. I am a member of the Image Computing & Analysis Lab, advised by Dr. Nathan Cahill, and funded by R01 NIH grant under PI Dr. David Borkholder. My PhD research involves math modeling of cochlear fluids, by leveraging atlas based image registration and segmentation, machine learning, and inverse optimization problems. My other research interest include deep learning on multi-modal imaging datasets, classical computer vision and image processing, and health care data science.

I have a Bachelors in Electronics and Communication from University Visvesvaraya College of Engineering, Bangalore University, India. My senior project in Digital Elevation Models was done at the Indian Space Research Organization, from where my interest in imaging science peaked. I pursued this interest and obtained a Master's in Electrical Engineering with concentration in Image and Signal Processing from Rochester Institute of Technology. With opportunity to be a Research Assistant on a grant project funded by Hewlett-Packard during Masters, it gave the perfect platform to continue to the next level and get a PhD.
I have a passion for rock climbing, cooking, photography and traveling.
